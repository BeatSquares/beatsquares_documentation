![Podcast preview](/images/podcast_preview.png)

Creating a podcast in BeatSquares follows a segment-based workflow that gives you granular control over each part of your episode. Unlike traditional podcast creation tools, BeatSquares lets you work on individual segments independently—generating, reviewing, and perfecting each one before moving to the next.

The process is built around segments as your primary building blocks. For each segment, you'll select articles, generate audio, choose from multiple AI-generated variants, and select background music. Once you're satisfied with a segment, you verify it and move on to the next. This segment-by-segment approach means you're never locked into decisions made early in the process—you can always go back and refine individual segments without affecting the rest of your podcast.

The workflow includes a special "overview" segment that can only be generated once all your content segments are verified. This ensures your introduction or summary accurately reflects your finalized content. When all segments are verified and you're happy with the results, you publish the podcast to generate the final audio file.

The entire process typically takes 15-20 minutes of active work per segment, with BeatSquares handling the AI generation in the background while you continue working on other segments or tasks.

---

## What You'll Learn

You will learn how to:
- Create a new podcast episode inside a Square
- Add articles to podcast segments
- Generate and review individual segment audio
- Select from AI-generated variants for each segment
- Choose background music for segments
- Verify segments when complete
- Generate overview segments
- Publish your finished podcast

---

## Before You Start

### Prerequisites
- A BeatSquares account with podcast features enabled
- At least one Square created with "Podcast" format
- Articles available in your data source

### What You'll Need
- A podcast concept and structure in mind
- Understanding of which articles you want to feature
- Time to review and refine each segment individually

## Creating Podcasts

### Start from the Dashboard

Go to the [BeatSquares Dashboard](https://app.beatsquares.com/).

Podcasts are created within Squares set to the output format **"Podcast."**

If you don't have a Square yet, [learn how to create one here](/core-features/creating-squares).

### Step 1. Open a Podcast Square

Click **View square** on a Square configured for podcast output to begin.

### Step 3. Name Your Podcast

Enter a name for your podcast episode. Podcasts cannot be saved without a name.

### Step 4. Add Articles to Segment

Each podcast is built from **segments**, and each segment is based on one or more **articles**.

Click **Add articles** to begin adding content to your first segment.

<Note>
You can add, remove, or reorder segments at any time during the creation process. Each segment can be worked on independently.
</Note>

### Step 5. Select Articles

A popup lets you choose articles for the segment.

- By default, you'll see articles from the same Square
- To use articles from other sources, check **Search for articles outside this square**

Select the articles you want to include in this segment, then click **Add to segment**.

### Step 6. Generate Segment

Once you've added articles to a segment, click **Generate** to create the audio for that specific segment.

<Tip>
Unlike the old workflow where all segments were generated at once, you can now generate, review, and perfect each segment individually before moving to the next one.
</Tip>

If you want more control, you can manually edit the segment's script. Click **Edit segment** to modify the text that will be converted to audio.

<Warning>
Manual edits will require regenerating the segment audio. Make sure you're satisfied with your edits before regenerating.
</Warning>

### Step 12. Verify Segment

When you're completely satisfied with a segment—including its content, variant selection, and background music—click **Verify segment**.

Verified segments are locked in and ready for the final podcast. You can always unverify and make changes if needed.

<Note>
The overview segment can only be generated once all content segments are verified. This ensures your introduction accurately reflects your finalized content.
</Note>

### Step 13. Repeat for All Segments

Repeat Steps 4-12 for each segment in your podcast:
1. Add articles
2. Generate segment
3. Play and review
4. Select best variant
5. Choose background music
6. Make any manual edits
7. Verify the segment

### Step 14. Generate Overview Segment

Once all your content segments are verified, you can generate the **Overview segment**. This is typically an introduction or summary that encompasses the entire episode.

Click **Generate overview** to create this special segment.

<Warning>
The overview segment will only generate successfully if all other segments have been verified. If you see an error, check that all content segments show a "Verified" status.
</Warning>

### Step 16. Publish Podcast

When all segments (including the overview) are verified and you're satisfied with the complete episode, click **Publish**.

This combines all verified segments into a single, finalized podcast file.

<Note>
Publishing generates the final audio file and marks your podcast as "Ready to Publish" in the Square overview.
</Note>

## Best Practices

- **Work Segment by Segment:** Focus on perfecting one segment at a time rather than trying to generate everything at once
- **Explore All Variants:** Always listen to all available variants before choosing one—the AI may surprise you with creative interpretations
- **Match Music to Content:** Use different background music for different segments to create variety and emotional resonance
- **Verify Strategically:** Only verify segments when you're completely satisfied, as this locks them in for the overview generation
- **Generate Overview Last:** Always generate and verify all content segments before creating your overview segment
- **Review Before Publishing:** Listen to the complete podcast at least once before downloading or sharing

## Understanding the Workflow

### Segment States

Segments move through several states:
1. **Draft:** Articles added, not yet generated
2. **Generated:** Audio created, ready for review
3. **Edited:** Manual changes made, may need regeneration
4. **Verified:** Finalized and locked in for publishing

### Why Verify Segments?

Verification serves two purposes:
- It signals that a segment is complete and ready for the final podcast
- It enables overview segment generation, which needs to know what content is finalized

### The Overview Segment

The overview segment is special because it:
- Can only be generated when all content segments are verified
- Typically serves as an introduction or summary
- Has access to information about all verified segments
- Should be generated and verified last

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Can't generate overview segment
**Solution:** Ensure all content segments are verified. Check each segment's status.

**Problem:** Variant selection disappeared after editing
**Solution:** Manual edits require regeneration, which creates new variants. You'll need to select from the new variants.

**Problem:** Background music not applying
**Solution:** Make sure you've saved your music selection before generating or regenerating the segment.

## Conclusion

You now understand BeatSquares' segment-based podcast workflow: create segments individually, generate and review audio, select from variants, choose background music, verify each segment, generate an overview when all segments are verified, publish the complete podcast, and download or share the final file.

This workflow gives you precise control over every part of your podcast, with the flexibility to perfect each segment independently before committing to the final product.
